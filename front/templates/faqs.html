Frequently asked questions

What is an Akiya?An akiya is a vacant or abandoned house in Japan, often available at very affordable prices.
Are only "akiyas" (abandoned houses) available on this site?No — we aggregate properties from multiple Japanese websites. Some are akiyas, while others are not.
Can foreigners buy property in Japan?Yes, foreigners can purchase property in Japan with no restrictions. Full ownership is yours.
Do I need to live in Japan to buy a house?No, you can complete the entire process remotely and visit Japan whenever you choose.
Does purchasing a house in Japan grant me a visa?No, Japan does not provide investor visas for real estate owners.
Can I use my property in Japan as an Airbnb?Yes, depending on the location (zoning and local rules), you can operate your property as a short-term rental.
Can I rent my property in Japan for long-term leases?Yes, you can.
What are the additional costs involved?Additional costs may include taxes and agent fees, depending on the property.

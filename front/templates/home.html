Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What is an Akiya?
A: An Akiya is a vacant or abandoned house in Japan, often available at very affordable prices.
Q: Are only "Akiyas" (abandoned houses) available on this site?
A: No, we aggregate properties from multiple Japanese websites. Some are Akiyas, while others are not.
Q: Can foreigners buy property in Japan?
A: Yes, foreigners can purchase property in Japan with no restrictions. Full ownership is yours.
Q: Do I need to live in Japan to buy a house?
A: No, you can complete the entire process remotely and visit Japan whenever you choose.
Q: Does purchasing a house in Japan grant me a visa?
A: No, Japan does not provide investor visas for real estate owners.
Q: Can I use my property in Japan as an Airbnb?
A: Yes, depending on the location, you can manage your property as an Airbnb.
Q: Can I rent my property in Japan for long-term leases?
A: Yes, you can.
Q: What are the additional costs involved?
A: Additional costs may include taxes, agent fees, depending on the property.
